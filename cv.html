Professional Biography

My name is Kunyu He, and I am an end-to-end AI Engineer currently completing a Master of Information Technology with a specialisation in Artificial Intelligence at The University of Western Australia. My journey in computing began at the University of Science and Technology Beijing, where I studied Artificial Intelligence and developed a strong grounding in machine learning, deep learning, and natural language processing. I graduated in June 2024 with a Bachelor of Engineering and a GPA of 3.5 out of 4.0, recognised along the way with the First Prize in the USTB Mathematical Competition and the Third Prize in the People's Scholarship — reflections of the analytical rigour I carry into every technical challenge.

A defining project during my undergraduate studies was a Natural Language Inference system for scientific texts. Working with over 26,000 text pairs, I engineered an end-to-end NLP pipeline implementing BiLSTM, ESIM, and Transformer architectures, then conducted systematic ablation studies on feature fusion strategies. This work lifted baseline accuracy from 66.27% to 74.08% — an experience that cemented my belief that disciplined experimentation, not intuition alone, is what moves the needle in applied AI.

My professional experience progressed from embedded and data roles into enterprise AI. At Baike Rongchuang in mid-2023, I programmed STM32 microcontrollers in C/C++ to support an automated cargo classification system and deployed an OCR model onto embedded hardware — my first experience closing the loop between a trained model and real-world deployment constraints. At Cummins (China) in late 2023, I built Regex-based rule libraries for automated PII data tagging and designed Power BI dashboards integrating Tenable cloud data, providing cross-departmental stakeholders with data-driven insight for security remediation.

My most substantive role to date is as a Consulting Intern at IBM (China), where since November 2025 I have been architecting enterprise-grade AI Agents and RAG pipelines. I spearheaded the full-lifecycle development of AI Agents using Volcengine Data Agent and HiAgent workflows, integrated the Model Context Protocol (MCP) to connect large language models with custom enterprise databases, and enabled hallucination-free financial insights through natural-language anomaly analysis. I also developed and deployed a hybrid supply chain AI system combining time-series and regression models — Croston, TSB, Random Forest, XGBoost — with LLM reasoning, and architected the full MCP-integrated deployment on Volcengine cloud for continuous automated decision support.

At UWA, my Master of IT studies in AI are deepening my capabilities in deep learning, NLP, and data science, while giving me the opportunity to build this four-page interactive web project for CITS5505 — a practical exercise in front-end engineering, AJAX-driven quiz logic, API integration, and accessible responsive design. Looking ahead, I am focused on architecting production-grade agentic AI systems that bridge research and enterprise impact. I welcome opportunities to contribute deep ML engineering, cloud deployment experience, and a bias for independent execution to a team solving high-value real-world problems.
